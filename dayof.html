This page contains information related to the day of the wedding itself. Read on to learn about the dress code, getting to the venue, the menu, and the homework assignment(s).

Directions to the Venue

See the Brooklyn Grange website for directions and accessibility information for the Sunset Park location. Do not go to the Navy Park location; we will not be there.

Brooklyn Grange, Sunset Park is a short (10-minute) walk from the 36th Street subway station, which is serviced by the N/R (yellow) and the D (orange) lines. It takes about 40 minutes to get there by subway from Times Square.

If arriving by car, we urge you to check estimated arrival times starting around 3 PM the day of. Depending on traffic conditions, the drive from downtown Manhattan could take between 30 minutes and 1 hour and 30 minutes.

The venue is on top of an aging strip mall that calls itself Liberty BKLYN. From the street, you'll walk along the building (passing a Micro Center and a Saks OFF 5TH) to the last set of doors, right after a Bed Bath & Beyond sign. The elevators to the roof are immediately to the left once you go through these doors. There will be signage and a Brooklyn Grange empolyee on the day to help direct.

Parking at the Venue

Parking at the venue will be street parking only; do not park in the lot in front of the venue. This parking lot closes on Fridays at 8 PM. After 8 PM, any vehicles remaining in the lot will be locked in until the following morning. There is usually street parking in the area with metered parking available under the Brooklyn-Queens Expressway (i.e., the Gowanus Expressway). Meters expire at 7 PM, after which parking is free.

Dress Code

For this event, the dress code is definitely not fully black tie, but it does lean towards the more formal end of things. Since dress code words seem to mean nothing consistent, don't hesitate to reach out with any questions.

The overarching idea is to dress up, treat yourself! Dresses do not need to be floor length, but pants very much should be. We'd love to see some bright colors and spirited patterns.

Suits with ties are welcome, but no need to go full tuxedo. If you wear a tuxedo, Tim will feel like he needs to wear a tuxedo, and Tim does not want to wear a tuxedo. Bring a jacket for pictures then use the jacket to claim your chair when dancing!

Heels are definitely not expected. The walkways are rocks. If you would like to wear heels, try them out on cobblestone first and see how you do.

If at all possible, please wear orange. Please.

Postcard Guest Book

We know the thing everyone is really the most excited for is signing the wedding guest book. At this wedding, we are making you work for it.

Please bring to the wedding a postcard of your choosing with your names (and whatever else you'd like!) written on it. We will collect these postcards at the wedding and bind them into a guest book made up of your contributions. There are no size or shape limitations for the postcards.
