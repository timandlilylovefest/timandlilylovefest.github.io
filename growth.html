Growing Tips

(Strictly of the Plant Variety)

Thank you again for celebrating with us at Brooklyn Grange! To serve as a memento of the night, the wedding favors contain a mix of the same seeds planted along the walkways of the venue. Below you'll find some more information about these seeds and some general plant-care advice.

Don't hesitate to reach out with any questions and please please please share any progress photos!

The packets contain a mix of the following seeds:

  • Calendula (Orange Flash)
  • Celosia (Pampas Plume)
  • Cosmos (Bright Lights)
  • Cosmos (Double Click Mix)
  • Zinnia (Queeny Formula Mix)

Planting

Seeds should be planted approximately 1/4" deep into moist but well-drained soil. If growing in a pot, stick to pots made of porous material like clays (e.g., terracotta, ceramic, etc.) rather than plastic. For better drainage, consider starting with a layer of gravel at the bottom of the pot before filling the rest with potting soil.

Watering

Before sprouts appear, moisten the soil whenever dry. Misting works well at this stage so as to not disturb the seeds. Once germinated, water the plants about once a week, making sure to use enough water to soak through the soil.

Sun

These plants like Sun. Try to give the plants, even as seeds before sprouting, as much Sun as possible. This will encourage growth and proper formation of flowers.

Temperature

Do not plant any seeds outside until after the last frost. The soil temperature, whether outside or in a pot, should stay above 60°F (16°C) at minimum. The plants will do best at temperatures in the 70s°F (21-27°C).

Maintenance

You can expect to start seeing sprouts between one and two weeks. The plants can be thinned after the first few leaves appear--remove some fraction of the plants, especially in overcrowded areas, to give the remaining plants more room to grow.

It always feels wrong, but you can pinch the plants to encourage branching and produce fuller plants. When the plants have a few sets of leaves, use your fingernails to pinch off the top pair of leaves on a stem. Pinch close to the next set of leaves down. Two stems should then grow from that set of leaves.
